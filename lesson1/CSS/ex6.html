Thai Panang Beef Curry

Panang Curry is a rich, creamy, and slightly sweet Thai red curry. It is less spicy than traditional red curry and features a thick coconut sauce with tender beef slices.

Ingredients

  • 400g Beef sirloin or flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 tablespoons Panang curry paste
  • 1 can (400ml) Coconut cream or thick coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons Fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Palm sugar (or regular brown sugar)
  • 4-5 Kaffir lime leaves, finely shredded
  • 1 Red chili, sliced (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon Cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a pan over medium heat, add the Panang curry paste, and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Slowly pour in half of the coconut cream (200ml) and stir well with the paste until a thin layer of red oil develops on top.
  3. Add the sliced beef into the pan and cook until it changes color and becomes tender.
  4. Pour in the remaining coconut cream, then season with fish sauce and palm sugar. Simmer gently for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Stir in half of the shredded kaffir lime leaves.
  6. Turn off the heat, transfer to a serving bowl, and garnish with the remaining lime leaves and fresh sliced red chili. Serve hot with steamed Jasmine rice!
